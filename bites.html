Brick Oven Pizza

My favorite type of pizza is pepperoni, but more specifically, brick oven pepperoni. The crust is thin and crispy, the cheese is bubbly and slightly charred, and the pepperoni is crispy and slightly curled. I personally love to top it all off with grated parmesan and crushed red pepper flakes to add a nice touch of heat and saltiness, but drizzling hot honey takes it to the next level.

What you'll need:

  • Pizza stone or cast iron pan
  • Water
  • Yeast
  • Sugar
  • Flour
  • Salt
  • Olive oil
  • Cornmeal
  • Sauce
  • Cheese
  • Toppings

Directions:

  1. Place pizza stone or cast iron pan in oven and preheat to 500°F (260°C) or the highest temperature possible. Allow 30-45 minutes for oven to heat up and closely mimic a brick oven.
  2. Mix warm water, yeast, and sugar together in a bowl. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until foamy.
  3. Add in flour, salt, and olive oil. Mix and knead for around 5 minutes or until smooth.
  4. Cover dough and allow it to rise in a warm setting for 30-45 minutes until it's doubled in size.
  5. Roll and stretch out dough on a floured surface. If using a pizza peel or the back of a baking sheet, lighty cover surface with cornmeal to prevent sticking.
  6. Dust dough with olive oil to assist with browning before applying sauce and toppings.
  7. Add a thin layer of your desired sauce, sprinkle cheese, and arrange toppings on the pizza.
  8. With caution, slide the pizza onto the heated stone or pan. Bake for 7-10 minutes until cheese is bubbly and crust becomes charred in places.
  9. For extra char, set oven to broil for the remaining 1-2 minutes. Just be sure to keep an eye on it!
  10. Allow pizza to sit for about 5 minutes, or until it's cooled to your liking, slice, and enjoy!

Spaghetti & Meat Sauce

Spaghetti and meat sauce is a classic comfort food that's easy to make and always satisfying. The combination of tender pasta and flavorful meat sauce is perfect for a cozy night in or a family dinner. You can customize the sauce with your favorite seasonings and add-ins to transform it into a versatile dish that everyone will love!

What you'll need:

  • Ground turkey or beef
  • Spaghetti sauce
  • Pasta
  • Oil
  • Seasonings

Directions:

  1. Lightly oil pan and heat stove to around medium heat. Bring water to a boil in pot.
  2. Add ground turkey to oiled pan once heated, season to your liking, and cook all the way through.
  3. Add pasta to boiling water and cook until al dente.
  4. Lower heat of the ground turkey to medium-low, add spaghetti sauce and season to your liking.
  5. Drain pasta, return to pot, and add oil prevent sticking
  6. Separate or combine noodles with meat sauce, pair with sides or enjoy alone.

Berry French Toast

French toast is my go-to breakfast entree that's perfect for any occasion. I'm always content with the classic version (extra powdered sugar), but sometimes I like to switch things up and add fresh berries for a fruity flavor. This berry french toast recipe takes the traditional dish to the next level by adding fresh strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries. The combination of sweet, eggy bread, syrup, sugar, and tart berries is a delicious treat that feels more like dessert than breakfast!

What you'll need:

  • Bread
  • Eggs
  • Milk
  • Sugar
  • Cinnamon
  • Berries
  • Syrup

Directions:

  1. Whisk together eggs, milk, sugar, and cinnamon in a bowl.
  2. Heat a skillet or griddle to medium heat and lightly oil.
  3. Dip bread slices into egg mixture, coating both sides.
  4. Place bread slices onto skillet or griddle and cook until golden brown on both sides.
  5. Serve with syrup, powdered sugar, and fruit.

Soy Garlic Sesame Wings

These soy garlic sesame wings are the perfect combination of sweet, savory, and umami flavors. The wings are marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, garlic, sesame oil, and honey, then baked until crispy and golden brown. They can be served as an appetizer, snack, or main dish, and I'd pair them with a side of white or fried rice and cucumbers for a refreshing contrast.

What you'll need:

  • Chicken wings
  • Soy sauce
  • Garlic
  • Sesame oil
  • Honey
  • Sesame seeds

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Mix together soy sauce, garlic, sesame oil, and honey in a bowl.
  3. Toss chicken wings in sauce and marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Place wings on a baking sheet and bake for 30-40 minutes, flipping halfway through.
  5. Garnish with sesame seeds, plate, and inhale because they're that good.

Roasted Rosemary Potatoes

I enjoy almost all forms of potatoes, especially those that are crispy and seasoned. Roasted rosemary potatoes are a simple and delicious side dish that pairs well with a host of main courses. The fresh rosemary adds an unforgettable fragrant and earthy flavor that makes this dish so different from simply salted and peppered potatoes.

What you'll need:

  • Potatoes
  • Olive oil
  • Rosemary
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Wash, pat dry, and cut potatoes into bite-sized pieces.
  3. Strip rosemary leaves from stems and chop.
  4. Toss potatoes with olive oil, rosemary, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  5. Spread potatoes on a baking sheet and bake for 30-40 minutes, flipping halfway through.
  6. Plate and enjoy!

Fish & Chips

Fish and chips is a comfort food that I don't eat often, but when I do, I surely savor it! The crispy beer batter is satisfying to eat and delicious, so much that if I do top the fish with anything, it's salt, pepper, and lemon. This recipe also contains one of my favorite forms of potatoes, chips, which are crispy and seasoned to perfection!

What you'll need:

  • Fish fillets
  • Beer
  • Flour
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Oil
  • Potatoes

Directions for fish:

  1. Preheat oil in a deep fryer or pot to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Mix together flour, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  3. Dip fish fillets in beer batter, shaking off excess.
  4. Dredge fish fillets in flour mixture, shaking off excess.
  5. Fry fish fillets for 4-6 minutes or until golden brown and cooked through.
  6. Serve with chips, your preferred condiments if any, and lemon wedges!

Directions for chips:

  1. Wash, peel, and cut potatoes into thin pieces.
  2. Soak potatoes in cold water for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat oil in a deep fryer or pot to 375°F (190°C).
  4. Drain potatoes and pat dry.
  5. Fry potatoes in batches for 4-5 minutes or until golden brown.
  6. Drain on paper towels and season well.
  7. Enjoy with your favorite condiments!
